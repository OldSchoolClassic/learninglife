Resources

Helpful links, curriculum ideas, forms, and tools to strengthen your homeschool experience.

Note: I do not receive any compensation for recommending these resources. These are simply things we have used and enjoyed, or plan to use based on others' recommendations. You are always free to use any curriculum, resource, or method you prefer.

Start-of-Year Forms

Each year, families homeschooling under the general homeschool law must submit the following forms to their superintendent or umbrella school. The deadline is August 1 for returning homeschoolers, or before you begin homeschooling for first-timers. You can start counting days as early as July 1 once paperwork is submitted.

Tip: Download PDFs to your files rather than opening in-browser to make them fillable.

Curriculum Recommendations

The majority of my recommendations are Christian-based because that's what we use in our home. For non-religious curriculum, I've heard good things about Time4Learning, which covers core subjects with month-to-month subscriptions.

Complete Curriculum

  • EasyPeasy All-in-One Homeschool — Free, online, based on PA requirements. A la carte or full curriculum. Free PDF printouts; pre-printed workbooks available.
  • The Good and the Beautiful — Core subjects K–12, literature incorporates geography. Free K–8 language arts and math. Video lessons included.

Learning to Read

  • Sing, Spell, Read, and Write — Our family favorite K–2. Check eBay for used copies. YouTube has all the songs. Workbooks and leveled readers are the key components.
  • All About Reading (AAR) — Multi-sensory, research-based phonics curriculum. Good for preschool through ~2nd grade. Appears non-religious.
  • Starfall — Free interactive K–5 platform. Multisensory, untimed games. Great for struggling or early readers.
  • SnapWords (K–4) — Flashcards with pictures drawn into sight words plus kinesthetic actions. Great for visual/kinesthetic learners.

Writing, Spelling & Handwriting

  • WriteShop — Hands-on writing curriculum. Higher parent involvement but strong long-term results.
  • Spelling You See (Demme Learning) — Levels A–G (~K–6). Teaches spelling concepts rather than memorization. Also improves reading.
  • PenTime Handwriting — Cursive through level 8. Cursive improves visual tracking and overall reading skills.

History & Literature

  • Notgrass History — Family-style Christian history grades 1–12 with literature, art, music, and hands-on projects.
  • Mystery of History — Biblical worldview, ancient to modern. Engaging narrative format.

Math

  • Simply Good and Beautiful Math (K–8) — Colorful spiral-review with video lessons. Free PDF downloads or inexpensive workbooks.
  • Saxon Math (9–12) — Highly recommended for high school. "My Math Assistant" companion site with videos and self-grading. Algebra 1 & 2 together cover geometry.
  • Math-U-See (K–12) — Visual, mastery-based. Uses manipulatives and videos. Goes all the way to calculus.
  • Math Worksheet Generator — Free tool to quickly create custom practice worksheets.
  • Musical Multiplication App (Good and the Beautiful) — Songs + gamification for memorizing multiplication facts. Up to 6 profiles per account.

Science

  • Science Shepherd — Christian-based, video instruction with workbooks. Good for independent learners.
  • Apologia — Biblical perspective, conversational tone, hands-on labs. Requires more parent involvement.

Bible

  • The Bible Project — Free, biblically accurate (non-denomination-specific) summary videos for every book plus round-table courses.
  • Answers in Genesis — Answers for life's toughest questions.
  • Apologetics Press — Excellent middle & high school resource covering faith and worldview tensions.

Other Resources

Homeschool Law

  • Ask Pauline — No longer updated but still a great PA homeschool law reference.
  • HSLDA — Home School Legal Defense Association. Advocates for families, offers membership scholarships and curriculum grants.

Reading Resources

  • ReadWorks (K–12) — Free high-quality literacy passages with paired texts and question sets. Audio options available. Spanish texts included.

College Prep

  • Saylor Academy — Free college courses transferable to Degree Completion Partners including Penn State World Campus.
  • OpenStax — Free peer-reviewed digital textbooks.

Grants & Scholarships

  • CHAP Foundation — PA nonprofit offering assistance for curriculum, unexpected expenses, and special needs homeschool costs.
  • HSLDA Compassion Grants — For curriculum, co-op tuition, testing fees. Also offers disaster relief and need-based memberships.
  • FAFSA / Pell Grant — Apply even if you're unsure you qualify — many colleges and loans require it.
  • HERO Scholarships, Sonlight Scholarships, Regent University, Study.com — Various homeschool-specific college scholarships. Also check directly with your college of choice.
