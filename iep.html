Students with IEPs

Support for students with diverse learning needs — guidance that honors each child's strengths and helps parents feel equipped and encouraged.

Do You Evaluate Students with Special Needs?

Yes! In fact, students with special needs make up a significant portion of homeschooled students. I hold a valid PA PK-12 special education certification, which qualifies me to evaluate students with active IEPs at all grade levels.

Personally, I am looking for progress that is appropriate for your child — not "grade level" performance. Every child's journey is unique, and my evaluations reflect that.

What the Law Says

If a child has an IEP through their school district, PA homeschool law requires that the evaluation be conducted by a licensed clinical or school psychologist, or a PA-certified teacher with a valid special education certificate. I meet this requirement.

This legal requirement only applies if the student currently has an active IEP. If a parent submits a written revocation of consent for the IEP, the child is no longer considered an "exceptional student" under IDEA for homeschool purposes.

Keeping vs. Revoking the IEP

It is your right to decide whether to maintain the IEP or revoke it. Neither choice affects your end-of-year evaluation. Here are some considerations:

If You Keep the IEP

  • Child continues to receive school-provided services (speech, OT, etc.)
  • Annual objectives must be pre-approved before registering each year
  • I do this for free — simply send me your objectives and I'll return the approval form
  • You can use vague, generic objectives — they will likely change throughout the year anyway

If You Revoke the IEP

  • Child is no longer considered an "exceptional student" under IDEA
  • District-provided therapies (speech, OT) will no longer be available through the school
  • If you later re-enroll in public school, a full psychological re-evaluation is required (up to 60 days)
  • Private therapies through health insurance remain available
Tip: If your child is benefiting from school-provided therapies, consider scheduling a first appointment with a private provider before revoking the IEP to prevent gaps in services.

Nonverbal Children

PA homeschool evaluations must include a student interview — but this does not require the child to speak. For nonverbal students, the interview can take the form of:

  • Observation of the student engaging with materials
  • Parent-facilitated interaction
  • Responses through gestures, body language, or AAC devices
  • Communication via tablets or communication boards
  • Parent-shared videos demonstrating how the child interacts and learns

The goal is simply to respectfully acknowledge the student as an active learner and document their progress. No verbal responses are necessary.

Please let me know ahead of time if your child is nonverbal so we can discuss how your child communicates before the evaluation. This allows us to cater the interview to their abilities and preferences.

Free IEP Objective Pre-Approvals

I don't think it should be harder or more expensive for families to homeschool children with special needs. That's why I offer IEP objective pre-approvals completely free of charge.

Simply send me your objectives and I'll return the approval form. You can use vague, generic objectives — there's no pressure to be overly specific, and you can change them any time without additional documentation.
