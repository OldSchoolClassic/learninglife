Graduation & Diplomas

Clear information on how Pennsylvania homeschool diplomas work, with practical steps to help your student move forward well-prepared and fully recognized.

Start planning in 7th grade. Many required sub-subjects can count toward graduation from 7th grade onward — but only if you keep records. I recommend beginning transcripts no later than 7th grade.

Diploma Options in Pennsylvania

Parent-Issued Diploma

Fully state-recognized since October 2014. A parent-issued diploma carries the same rights and privileges as an organization-issued one — accepted for college, employment, and scholarships (except organization-specific). This is a completely valid option.

PHAA Diploma

Pennsylvania Homeschoolers Accreditation Agency — statewide, includes online courses and evaluation services. Requires proficiency in reading, writing, and public speaking. Evaluation letters can serve as college recommendation letters.

ECHA Diploma

Erie County Homeschoolers Diploma Association — inclusive, state-recognized, with standards closely aligned to PA homeschool law. Standards are rigorous but accessible for diverse educational philosophies.

Other Organizations

Aleithia Learning Community, Susquehanna Valley Homeschool Diploma Programs, Bridgeway Academy, and Mason Dixon Homeschool Association also offer PA diploma programs. Each has its own requirements.

Note: The PA Department of Education does not directly issue diplomas to homeschoolers. It recognizes and approves organizations that provide diploma programs. If you choose an organization-issued diploma, familiarize yourself with their requirements by 9th grade — some have enrollment cut-off dates.

Graduation Requirements for a Parent-Issued Diploma

To issue a valid, state-recognized diploma, the student must complete the following while in compliance with the home education program:

4 Years — English

Must include language, literature, speech, and composition.

3 Years — Math

Must include general mathematics, algebra, and geometry. Trigonometry is recommended.

3 Years — Science

Biology and chemistry are preferred by colleges, but any science is acceptable.

3 Years — Social Studies

Must cover civics, U.S. & Pennsylvania history, and world history. Economics is recommended.

2 Years — Arts & Humanities

Art, music, foreign language, theatre, dance, philosophy — any aspect of culture qualifies. Two years of foreign language is often preferred by colleges.

Required Sub-Subjects (Grades 7–12, at some point)

English

  • Language & Literature
  • Speech
  • Composition

Math

  • General Mathematics
  • Algebra I
  • Geometry

Social Studies

  • Civics
  • World History
  • U.S. & Pennsylvania History

Also Required at Some Point

  • Science (biology & chemistry preferred)
  • Geography
  • Art
  • Music
  • Physical Education
  • Health & Safety Education (including fire prevention)
Sub-subjects covered as early as 7th grade can count toward graduation. For instance, if you covered geography in 7th grade, you don't need to cover it again in high school — but you do need documentation. This is why I recommend saving portfolios from 7th grade onward.

12th Grade Evaluation & Diploma Signature

The 12th grade evaluation is more in-depth than other years. For me to sign off on a PA-issued diploma, I need evidence that all required subjects were completed. This generally means bringing:

  • Transcripts (parent-created or from a school) for grades 7–12, or
  • Portfolio samples for each required secondary subject
  • Course descriptions are helpful but not always required

The diploma must be on the standardized form developed by the PA Department of Education (PDE) and must be signed by the 12th-grade evaluator (me) confirming the student's suitability for graduation.

Can my child graduate early? Yes! Under PA homeschool law, students may graduate before age 18 if they have completed all required subjects. Courses completed in grades 7 and 8 can count toward graduation. I'm happy to review your records and confirm requirements have been met.
