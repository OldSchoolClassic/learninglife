Start Homeschooling in Pennsylvania

Paperwork, IEP guidance, and first-year essentials — all in one place.

Schedule a Consult
Good news: You do not need your district's approval to begin homeschooling. You may begin immediately. You must have your beginning-of-year documentation submitted within 10 days of starting. The deadline for returning homeschoolers is August 1.

Starting at the Beginning of the Year

If beginning before August 1, submit to your superintendent:

  • A notarized Affidavit or Unsworn Declaration (no notary needed)
  • Educational Objectives (K–6 or 7–12)
  • Medical Services & Dental report or Exemption Form

You can start counting school days as early as July 1 once paperwork is submitted.

Starting Mid-Year

Switching mid-year is perfectly fine. Follow the same steps, plus:

  • Notify the school by submitting your affidavit and objectives
  • Submit a Withdraw Form to your current school (they may have their own form)
  • Begin keeping a portfolio from day one of homeschooling
  • Save samples of prior schoolwork to establish a portfolio baseline

Start-of-Year Forms Due August 1 for returning families · Before you begin for first-year families

Download fillable PDFs to your device first (rather than opening in browser) to make them fillable. Submit to your superintendent or umbrella school. Many returning homeschoolers submit paperwork together with their end-of-year evaluation to simplify things.

Reading Log Template

A reading log is simply a list of texts used during the year — books read independently, read-alouds, and audiobooks. Include title and author (or publisher for textbooks).

There's no minimum, but younger students typically log 30+ picture books. Use this template or document however you prefer.

Hours & Days Tracking

Elementary students need 900 hours or 180 days; secondary students need 990 hours or 180 days. Most families track days.

There is no well-defined legal definition of a "school day." Pennsylvania courts have ruled that learning happens every day — your signed statement that you met the requirement is sufficient. I do not require attendance documentation during evaluations.
